These user journeys map the primary pathways through the new ISC website, focusing on how our key audiences accomplish their goals.

### Journey 1: The Operator (Community Focus)

*   **Persona:** Sarah, a Senior Network Engineer.
*   **Goal:** Find the administrator's reference manual for the latest stable version of BIND 9.
*   **Context:** She's in her terminal, planning a server upgrade, and needs the definitive source of truth quickly. She is highly intolerant of marketing funnels.

**Flow:**
1.  **Entry Point:** Google search for "BIND 9 ARM" or navigates directly to `isc.org`.
2.  **Homepage:** She completely ignores the hero section and her eyes immediately scan for "Resources" or "Documentation" in the main navigation.
3.  **Page: `/resources/` (Resource Library):** She lands on the community hub. The large search bar is the first thing she interacts with. She types "Administrator Reference Manual 9.18".
4.  **Action:** The live search provides instant results. She clicks the top link.
5.  **Page: `/resources/doc/bind/9.18/arm/`:** She arrives at the exact documentation page. The page is clean, the typography is readable, and code snippets are well-formatted.
6.  **Outcome:** Sarah finds the information she needs in under 30 seconds. The site has reinforced her trust in ISC as a reliable, no-nonsense resource.

### Journey 2: The Decision-Maker (Enterprise Focus)

*   **Persona:** David, a CTO at a regional bank.
*   **Goal:** Evaluate whether ISC can provide reliable, expert support for their business-critical DNS infrastructure.
*   **Context:** His team has identified a risk with their current unsupported BIND setup. He needs a trustworthy, professional partner to mitigate this risk.

**Flow:**
1.  **Entry Point:** Lands on the **Homepage** via a recommendation or a search for "commercial BIND support".
2.  **Homepage:** The headline "The Trusted Foundation of the Internet" resonates. He immediately sees and clicks the **"For the Enterprise"** CTA.
3.  **Page: `/enterprise/` (Enterprise Gateway):** He scans the page, absorbing key messages about reliability, direct access to experts, and business continuity. He sees logos of other reputable companies.
4.  **Page: `/enterprise/support/`:** He clicks to learn more about support. He reviews the support tiers, noting the 24/7 availability and direct access to senior engineers.
5.  **Page: `/about/experts/`:** Curious, he navigates to the "Our Experts" page. He is impressed by the credentials and experience of the team. This builds immense confidence.
6.  **Action:** He navigates back to the support page and clicks the prominent **"Request a Quote"** button.
7.  **Form:** He fills out the clear, professional form with details about his infrastructure.
8.  **Outcome:** David feels confident that he has found the right partner. The website experience was professional, informative, and guided him directly to a conversion.

### Journey 3: The Mobile User (Quick Action)

*   **Persona:** A security researcher on the go.
*   **Goal:** Report a potential security vulnerability she has discovered.
*   **Context:** She is away from her desk and needs to find the proper reporting channel quickly and securely from her phone.

**Flow:**
1.  **Entry Point:** Google search on mobile for "ISC security contact". The top result is the Contact page.
2.  **Page: `/contact/` (on mobile):** The page loads instantly. She scrolls past general and sales contact info to a clearly labeled section: **"Report a Security Vulnerability"**.
3.  **Action:** The section provides a dedicated email address and instructions, possibly including a PGP key for encrypted communication.
4.  **Outcome:** The mobile experience was fast and efficient. The researcher easily found the critical information, enabling her to follow the correct procedure for responsible disclosure.
